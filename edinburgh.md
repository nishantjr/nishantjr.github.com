Reached Edinburgh at 4pm from Stroud.
After a quick view of Edinbrough castle and checking into the hostel,
I headed straight to the bike shop to pick up bottles and pressure up the tires.
The attendant was quite tickled by the granny gear I have on the Brompton.
It had travelled pretty well give or take an out-of-tension spoke.
The canvas bag was working out quite well, though it had brought along a fair amount
of airport and railway station dirt.

The late Scottish sunset gave me a a few hours to go on a quick running tour of
the city and Authur's seat---breath-taking views, of castles, cliffs, and the sea.
This sounds like the perfect place for a bicycle tour.
Unfortunately, the late sunset did not mean the restraunts stayed open late as well.
I did get a filling meal at the British institution of Nandos though.
Packed bags and an attempt at sleep with the machine-gun snores of my dorm-mates.
