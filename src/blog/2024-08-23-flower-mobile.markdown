I've been working on a mobile quite different from once I've worked on before.
Mobiles typically conjure images of delicately counter-balanced forces,
this one embraces assymetry and imbalance...

Previously, my mobiles have been see-saws, more-or-less balanced and counter-balanced.
While they weren't necessarily symmetric at all, lighter peices counter-balanced
much heavier ones at the end of a long lever.
Small bobs in the heavy end, must be counter balanced by wild swings in the lighter.
This makes for a great contract is motion---the light parts of the mobile dart and swirl,
while the heavier bits plod gently, gracefully, hypnotizingly.
(These are great for sitting under, perhaps while lounging on a sofa).

When balanced each of the supports of the mobile become roughly flat,
and small imbalances give proportional deviations from the
horizontal---though this can be quite sensitive when dealing with very assymetric pieces.

In this case, I use a heavy counter-balance to hold a small weight nearly vertically.

Unfortunately, it turns out that heavy counter-balances are unsightly and unwieldy.
Can we get rid of it?
But do we need gravity at all?
Can one not push a see-saw down the the force of your hands rather than sitting on it?
